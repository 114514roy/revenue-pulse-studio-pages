Paywall guide

IAP paywall not converting? Your paid boundary may be invisible.

If users can open the app but do not pay, the problem may not be price. The paid result may be too vague, too late in the workflow, or already solved by the free preview.

Do not start by changing the price

A weak paywall often survives price changes because the buyer still cannot name what they get after paying. Lowering a vague unlock from $9 to $2 does not make it concrete.

Start by naming the paid artifact: export, report, proof bundle, audit trail, deadline packet, saved decision, unlocked workflow, or finished deliverable.

Paid-boundary checks

The free preview already solves the painful job

If the free tier gives away the core result, the paid tier becomes convenience. Convenience can sell, but only when the user repeats the job often enough.

The unlock is named like a feature

Words like premium, unlimited, advanced, and pro describe your menu, not the buyer's outcome. Replace them with the actual result the buyer keeps.

The paywall appears before trust exists

AI-coded apps need extra trust signals: a sample output, clear privacy boundary, no account surprise, and a screenshot that proves the final result.

The first screenshot does not show the paid result

If the paid artifact is not visible on the App Store page, many users never learn why the unlock exists.

A better paywall sentence

Use this structure before editing UI:

Pay to create or keep [specific artifact] when [buyer moment] so you can [avoid loss or finish job].

For example: pay to export a timestamped deposit evidence packet before move-out so you can dispute deductions with organized proof. That is stronger than pay for unlimited photos.

Boundary

This guide does not promise conversion lift, sales, rankings, or App Review approval. It is a diagnosis aid for deciding whether your IAP, subscription, or paid download has a visible buying reason.
